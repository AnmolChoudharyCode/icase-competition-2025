Competition Details

Everything you need to know about participating

The Initiative

The Initiative ignites the smartest minds to further sharpen their business acumen and develop innovative solutions.

Round 1 (Initial Round Case Solution):

Participants will be provided a case, to which they will be required to send their solution PPT. Time to put those MECE skills to good use! Further details, criteria for evaluation for the same will be made known in due course. The top 10 participants will make it to round 2.

Submission Details: The PPT can have up to 8slides (+3 appendix), excluding the cover slides. Brevity is appreciated.

Round 2 (Final Round Presentation):

The final round will be the online case presentation, where the participants battle it out to showcase the brilliance of their solutions to an elite panel of judges.

The presentation will be virtual for 10 minutes, followed by a 5-minute Q&A session.

Notes and ground rules:

Following are the notes and ground rules:

  • The format and structure of the event could be subject to change and will be promptly communicated to the participants.
  • All decisions in general matters, judgments, and any matter pertaining to this event will be with the organizing team, and the Jury. The decisions of the organizing team will be final and binding on all participants

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about registration and participation

How do I register for the competition?

Registration is now open! Visit our registration section to register your team. You'll need to provide team details, member information, and institutional affiliation.

Go to Registration

What is the team size requirement?

Teams must consist of 2-4 members. All team members must be from the same institution. Cross-institutional teams are not allowed.

Is there a registration fee?

No, participation in the iCase Competition is completely free. All costs are covered by the organizing institution.

What materials will be provided?

Registered teams will receive the case study materials, guidelines, and submission templates via email 24 hours before the competition begins.

Can I participate individually?

No, this is a team-based competition. Individual participation is not allowed. You must form a team of 2-4 members.

What is the evaluation criteria?

Teams will be evaluated on analytical depth (30%), innovative solutions (25%), presentation skills (25%), and practical feasibility (20%).
